## 5. INTERVIEW PREP — 3 Q&A (STAR Method)

### Q1: How do you handle a student who says "I'm just bad at math"?

**S:** One of my trades apprentices at Cloverdale told me straight up he'd never been good at numbers.
**T:** He needed math to pass his apprenticeship but had given up before starting.
**A:** I started with problems I knew he could solve — simple applied stuff from his trade. Every right answer built a little more confidence. Then I slowly added difficulty.
**R:** By mid-semester he was coming to sessions early. He passed his math requirement.

### Q2: You'd tutor math, chem, and physics — which is your strongest?

**S:** Math is my strongest — I tutor it daily at university level.
**T:** But I also taught physics and chemistry to secondary students for 2 years back home.
**A:** I review the BC curriculum for chem/physics before each session so I'm always prepared. If a question ever falls outside my scope, I'm honest about it and come back with the answer next session.
**R:** Students trust me because I'm upfront and I always deliver.

### Q3: How do you keep students engaged during a full session?

**S:** High school students lose focus fast, especially after a full school day.
**T:** A 60-minute session where I lecture the whole time would lose anyone.
**A:** I break sessions into 15-minute blocks — instruction, guided practice, independent work, then review. I mix up the activity type so they stay engaged.
**R:** Students stay focused, retain more, and don't dread coming to tutoring.
