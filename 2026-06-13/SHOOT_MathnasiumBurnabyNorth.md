**T:** I want to work somewhere that has a proven teaching method and supports instructors with training.
**A:** Mathnasium's structured approach and focus on building confidence aligns perfectly with how I tutor naturally — break it down, build it up.
**R:** I can grow as an instructor while helping K-12 students the same way I've helped university students.

### Q2: Tell me about a time a student was struggling and how you helped.

**S:** A first-year calculus student came to the Learning Centre failing their midterms.
**T:** They were overwhelmed and didn't know where to start — every problem looked like a wall.
**A:** I started with the simplest concept they did understand, then built up step by step. We used practice problems and visual aids. After 4 sessions, they passed their next exam.
**R:** The student finished the course with a C+ and told me I was the reason they didn't drop out.

### Q3: How do you handle multiple students at once (small group setting)?

**S:** In the Learning Centre, I often have 2-3 students at different levels simultaneously.
**T:** Each needs different help — one with algebra, one with calculus.
**A:** I give each student a problem to attempt independently while I work with the other, rotating every 5-10 minutes.
**R:** All students get attention, build independence, and I stay efficient.
