# INFILTRATION LAYER — "They See Me as One of Them"
## Core Kernel Module | Company-DNA Mimicry at Every Stage
## ATS → Phone → Video → In-Person → Orientation → Hallways

---

### PRINCIPLE

> At every touchpoint — from the ATS parsing your resume to the moment
> you walk the halls on Day 30 — the goal is the same: **they see you
> and think "this is one of us."** Not "this person is qualified."
> Not "this person has relevant experience." **"This person belongs here."

This module transforms every output of the JOBS OS to achieve that.
It sits on top of all other modules and rewires their output through
the company's DNA filter.

### 1. COMPANY DNA EXTRACTION

For every JD, extract these from the job posting + company research:

#### Language Registry
Pull the top 20-30 words/phrases from the JD. These are the company's
internal language. Use them verbatim in resume, cover letter, and
interview answers. This is how ATS filters work — they scan for
exact phrase matches.

**Example — Amazon BD JD:**
```
mechanisms, reporting frameworks, tooling, KPIs, cross-functional,
vendor managers, scalable, executive presence, data-driven analytics,
category teams, incremental business, repeatable processes,
accountability frameworks, decision-making processes, business
development roadmap, stakeholder alignment, strategic initiatives,
continuous improvement, business insights, buy-in
```

#### Value System
What does this company worship? Extract from:
- Their "About Us" page
- Their leadership principles / values page
- Their interview process descriptions
- Their public statements (earnings calls, blog posts, press)

**Example — Amazon:**
- Customer obsession (everything starts with the customer)
- Ownership (think long-term, never say "not my job")
- High standards (excellent is table stakes)
- Frugality (do more with less)
- Bias for action (speed matters)
- Dive deep (data, data, data)

#### Cultural Cues
How do they actually behave?

| Dimension | Amazon | Google | Startup | Bank |
|-----------|--------|--------|---------|------|
| Communication | Direct, blunt | Collaborative, "Googley" | Casual, Slack-first | Formal, email-chain |
| Decision speed | Fast, bias for action | Data-heavy, consensus | Move fast, break things | Slow, risk-averse |
| Meeting style | 6-pager, read-first | Deck-heavy, debate | Standup, async | Presentation, hierarchy |
| Dress | Smart casual (Canada) | Casual (hoodies) | Very casual | Suit (client-facing) |
| Hierarchy | Flat-ish but PIP culture | Flat with titles | No titles | Strict hierarchy |

#### Anti-Patterns
What gets you immediately flagged as "not one of us"?

**Example — Amazon:**
- Presenting with slides (they write 6-pagers)
- Vague answers without data
- "At my last company we did it this way"
- Saying "that's not my job"
- Blaming others or external factors
- Being unprepared for "why Amazon?"

### 2. ATS INFILTRATION

#### Keyword Matrix
Build from the Language Registry. Place these at EXACT 2-4% density.

| Placement | Density | Where |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Summary | 4-5 keywords | First 3 lines — ATS scans top-heavy |
| Each bullet | 1-2 keywords | Context, not stuffed |
| Skills section | ALL keywords | This is where ATS matches hard |
| Cover letter | 3-4 keywords | First paragraph |

#### Title Alignment
Your resume title MUST mirror their job title, not your actual title.

| Their Title | Your Resume Title |
|-------------|------------------|
| BD Manager | Business Development Manager |
| Director of Ops | Director of Operations |
| Head of RevOps | Head of Revenue Operations |

DO NOT use your real title if it doesn't match. The ATS scores title
match heavily. Use their title. If asked in interview, say "my actual
title was Director of Operations, but the scope maps directly to
Business Development Manager."

#### Bullet Point Alchemy
Apply the company's value system to YOUR bullets:

**Your real story:**
"Built scheduling system across 32 locations"

**Generic good bullet:**
"Designed scheduling cadence and resource allocation across 32 locations"

**Amazon-infiltrated bullet:**
"Owned end-to-end design of scheduling mechanisms across 32 locations —
established KPIs, reporting frameworks, and accountability systems that
drove 40% improvement — insisted on highest standards with 100% quality"

Can you see it? Every Amazon LP word is woven in: "Owned" (Ownership),
"mechanisms" (their word), "KPIs" (their word), "reporting frameworks"
(their word), "accountability" (their word), "40% improvement" (Deliver
Results), "highest standards" (Insist on Highest Standards).

#### Format Mimicry
Match their internal doc format. If they write 6-pagers, keep your
resume text-dense with no fluff. If they're creative, add subtle
design. Default: single-column, text-only, ATS-parseable.

### 3. PHONE SCREEN MIMICRY

#### Language Mirroring
In the first 60 seconds, use 3-5 of their Language Registry words.
This subconsciously flags you as "speaks our language."

**Bad opening:**
"I managed operations across 32 locations and helped the company grow."

**Amazon-infiltrated opening:**
"I built scalable mechanisms and reporting frameworks across 32 locations,
owning end-to-end operational infrastructure. We drove continuous
improvement through data-driven decision-making and accountability
frameworks that scaled from 3 to 70."

#### Pacing & Energy Match
- **Amazon:** Fast, direct, energetic. Don't pause too long. Be decisive.
- **Consulting:** Polished, structured, measured. Use frameworks.
- **Startup:** Casual, excited, conversational. Be authentic.
- **Bank:** Formal, deliberate, respectful. Don't interrupt.

Match their pace within 30 seconds. If they speak fast, speed up.
If they're measured, slow down. Mirroring builds subconscious rapport.

#### First 30 Seconds Script
```
[Greeting] + [Who I Am in THEIR words] + [Why THIS company] + [Hook]
```

**Example — Amazon:**
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time. I'm an operations leader who
specializes in building scalable mechanisms and reporting frameworks
that let cross-functional teams execute better. Amazon's Day 1 culture
and obsession with data-driven decision-making is exactly the
environment where I do my best work. I've spent 8 years building
exactly what this BD role needs — mechanisms, KPIs, accountability
systems — and I'm excited to bring that to Amazon Canada."

#### Cultural References
Drop 1-2 references that show you've done your homework.

**Amazon:**
"I read about Amazon's Canada expansion strategy and the focus on
Hardlines — the CE category seems like a huge growth opportunity."
"Your approach to written narratives instead of slide decks really
resonates — I've always believed clear writing forces clear thinking."

### 4. VIDEO INTERVIEW INFILTRATION

#### Visual Stage
| Element | Amazon | Consulting | Startup |
|---------|--------|------------|---------|
| Background | Clean, neutral wall or bookshelf | Virtual background or tidy home office | Neutral but real (kitchen/living room OK) |
| Lighting | Bright, face well-lit | Professional ring light | Natural light OK |
| Camera | Eye level, stable | Eye level, good webcam | Laptop camera OK but eye level |
| Attire | Collared shirt (no tie) | Suit jacket | T-shirt or hoodie |

#### THEIR-Context Story Framing
Every story must be framed in THEIR context, not yours.

**Your story:** "Built team from 3 to 70"
**Generic framing:** "I hired and trained 70 people"
**Amazon framing:** "I owned end-to-end organizational scaling —
designed hiring mechanisms, training infrastructure, and accountability
systems that grew the team from 3 to 70 while maintaining 100% quality
standards. This meant I couldn't accept mediocrity — every hire had to
be a bar raiser for the team."

See it? "Owned" (LP), "mechanisms" (their word), "accountability
systems" (their word), "bar raiser" (Amazon-specific term).

#### Live Language Calibration
During the interview, listen for their specific words and start using
them back. If the interviewer says "scalable mechanisms," you say
"scalable mechanisms" in your next answer. If they say "vendor
partnerships," mirror it. This builds instant rapport.

### 5. IN-PERSON IMMERSION

#### Dress Code Map
| Company Type | What to Wear | What NOT to Wear |
|-------------|--------------|------------------|
| Amazon (office) | Smart casual: dark jeans, blazer or collared shirt, clean shoes | Suit, tie, hoodie |
| Amazon (warehouse) | Safety shoes, high-vis vest (if touring) | Anything clean/fancy |
| Consulting | Suit, tie, dress shoes | Casual wear |
| SaaS startup | Clean casual: dark jeans, t-shirt or sweater, sneakers | Suit, anything too formal |
| Bank/corp | Suit, tie, polished shoes | Casual, no jacket |

#### Body Language Sync
- **Amazon:** Confident, direct eye contact, decisive hand gestures.
  Don't fidget. Show energy.
- **Consulting:** Polished, controlled, minimal gestures. Steepling
  fingers = confident.
- **Startup:** Relaxed, open posture, lean forward. Be engaged but
  casual.
- **Bank:** Formal posture, sit straight, controlled movements.

#### Office Navigation
- Arrive 10-15 min early
- Observe the vibe when you walk in: quiet and focused? Loud and
  collaborative? Match it.
- Use the bathroom before the interview (gives you 2 min to observe
  the office culture)
- Make small talk with the receptionist — they often feed back to
  the hiring team

#### Pre/Post Meeting Scripts

**With receptionist/coordinator:**
"How long have you been with the company? What do you like about
working here?" (Shows genuine interest, builds allies)

**To the interviewer as you walk to the room:**
"Love the office — the energy here is [observation]."
(Makes you seem like you already fit)

**Post-interview with coordinator:**
"Everyone's been incredibly welcoming. Really impressed by the
[observation about culture]."

### 6. ORIENTATION READINESS

#### Pre-Learned Internal Lingo
Before Day 1, know:
- What they call their internal tools (Quips? Slack? Teams?)
- Acronyms (PIP, QBR, OKR, LP, WBR, MBR)
- Department names (they're "Retail Hardlines" not "Sales")
- Leadership names (CEO, SVP, your VP, your director)

#### Org Chart Awareness
Know before Day 1:
- Who reports to whom (at least 2 levels above you)
- Who the key cross-functional partners are
- Which teams you'll work with most

**How:** LinkedIn search + company website + news articles.

#### System Familiarity
Before Day 1, research (not master, just know):
- What CRM/ERP they use (Salesforce? Oracle? Internal tool?)
- What communication tools (Slack? Teams? Email-heavy?)
- What their internal knowledge base is called

#### First Week Scripts

**To your manager:**
"Before I start, I've been reading about [specific initiative the
team is working on]. I have some thoughts on how we might approach
the reporting framework. Happy to share when I'm settled in."

**To a teammate:**
"I've been looking at the current [tool/report] and noticed
[observation]. Is that something the team has been thinking about?"

**Goal:** You sound like someone who's already thinking like they do.

### 7. HALLWAY PASS — Mistaken for Employee

#### Natural Presence
By Day 30, colleagues should forget you're new. How:
- Learn names fast (repeat them immediately when introduced)
- Know the coffee machine, bathroom, meeting room locations
- Have opinions on the office (the coffee, the temperature, the chairs)
- Ask questions that show you're thinking about the work, not the job

#### 30-Day "Been Here Months" Vibe

| Week | Vibe | Actions |
|------|------|---------|
| 1 | "New but sharp" | Listen, learn, ask good questions |
| 2 | "Catches on fast" | Start contributing in meetings |
| 3 | "Already useful" | Deliver first small win |
| 4 | "Feels like they've been here" | Independent, suggesting improvements |
| 8 | "How were we ever without them?" | Owning mechanisms, trusted partner |

#### Mistaken Identity Protocol
When someone mistakes you for a longer-tenured employee — which will
happen if you do this right — don't correct them with "I just started."
Say: "Thanks — I'm glad it feels that way. I've been focused on
[quick win] and really enjoying the team." This reinforces the
perception.
