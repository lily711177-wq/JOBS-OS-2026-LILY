# BACKGROUND CHECK PREPARATION
## Employment Verification, Education Checks, and PR Questions

---

### WHAT THEY CHECK (Director Level)

| Check Type | What They Verify | Risk Level |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| Employment | Company, title, dates — via third-party or direct call | Medium |
| Education | Degrees, institutions, graduation dates | Low |
| Criminal Record | Canadian Police Information Centre (CPIC) | Low (if clean) |
| Credit Check | Only for finance-adjacent roles | Low |
| Reference | Manager + peer calls | Medium |
| Social Media | LinkedIn, public profiles | Medium |

---

### EMPLOYMENT VERIFICATION STRATEGY

**The Challenge:**
Your last company was a US healthcare B2B SaaS that was acquired. The company may no longer exist under the same name. Your title was COO — but if Workday encounters a different title in their system, it flags.

**Solutions:**

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Company was acquired, name changed | Provide W2s, tax documents, or a letter from former CEO |
| Title mismatch (resume vs. payroll) | Provide offer letter from the time showing COO title |
| Cross-border US→Canada verification | Provide Canadian-side documentation if available |
| Gap between roles (Chief of Staff → COO) | Internal promotion — natural, explain in 1 sentence |

**Documents to prepare:**
- [ ] Offer letter from original company (shows start date + title)
- [ ] Promotion letter (shows COO title + effective date)
- [ ] W2 or T4 for each year of employment
- [ ] LinkedIn recommendation from CEO (corroborates your story)
- [ ] News/article about the acquisition (shows $17M exit)
- [ ] LinkedIn profile from the time (if archived)

**Script if background check flags something:**
> "The company was acquired in [year], which may cause a discrepancy in how the entity is listed. I can provide [specific document] that confirms my employment. Happy to connect you with my former CEO as well."

---

### EDUCATION VERIFICATION

- MBA: Ensure institution is recognized in Canada
- BSc IT: Same
- KPU Diploma: This is already a Canadian institution — easiest to verify

**Have ready:**
- Transcripts (official if possible)
- Diploma copies
- WES evaluation (if applicable for international degrees)

---

### CRIMINAL RECORD CHECK

- [ ] Get a police information check (PIC) from Vancouver Police Department proactively
- [ ] Have it ready to share (less than 6 months old)
- [ ] If you have a record, disclose it early and honestly

---

### SOCIAL MEDIA CLEANUP

**Before applications go out:**
- [ ] LinkedIn matches resume exactly (title, dates, company names)
- [ ] No controversial posts visible
- [ ] Profile photo is professional, neutral
- [ ] Privacy settings: limit public visibility of personal posts
- [ ] Remove any posts that reference healthcare specifically (if pivoting industry)
- [ ] Check for tagged photos — untag anything unprofessional

---

### VISA/BACKGROUND CHECK INTERSECTION

Some background check companies verify immigration status. Be prepared:

**If they ask about work permit status during background check:**
> "As discussed during my interviews, I hold an Open Work Permit with full authorization to work in Canada. My PR application is in process through economic streams. I can provide my SIN, work permit copy, and AOR from IRCC."

**Documents to provide:**
- Copy of valid work permit
- SIN number
- Acknowledgment of Receipt (AOR) from IRCC for PR
- Passport bio page
- Canadian driver's license or provincial ID

---

### TIMELINE OF CHECKS

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| Before applying | Gather all documents, request CEO letter |
| Week 1-2 | Complete social media cleanup |
| Week 3-4 | Get police check done (valid 6 months) |
| Week 5+ (offer stage) | Provide documents promptly when requested |
| After acceptance | Follow up to confirm all checks cleared |
