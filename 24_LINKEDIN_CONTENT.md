# LINKEDIN CONTENT STRATEGY
## Build Thought Leadership While You Job Search

---

### THE STRATEGY
Post 2x/week about operations, scaling, and leadership.
Position yourself as a practitioner who shares real experience — not a job seeker looking for handouts.

---

### CONTENT PILLARS

| Pillar | Topics | Audience |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Scaling Systems** | What breaks when you grow from 30→100→300 people, how to fix it | Ops leaders, founders |
| **Revenue Operations** | Sales cycles, CRM, forecasting, NRR | RevOps, Sales leaders |
| **Strategic Planning** | OKRs, planning rhythms, board reporting | Strategy, execs |
| **Leadership** | Building teams, remote culture, decision-making | Managers, directors |

---

### POST TEMPLATES (Mix and Match)

**Post Type 1: "Here's what I learned" (Experience share)**
> "When I joined my last company, there were 3 of us and no playbooks.
>
> When we exited, we had 120 people, $4M ARR, and systems for everything.
>
> The 3 systems I built first (and why they mattered most):
>
> 1. [System 1 — e.g., Hiring infrastructure]
>    We couldn't grow until we could hire reliably. So I built the pipeline from scratch — role definitions, comp bands, interview process, onboarding. Result: 40x headcount growth in 24 months.
>
> 2. [System 2 — e.g., Revenue operations]
>    Without CRM discipline, forecasting was guessing. So I built the architecture. Result: 40% shorter sales cycle, 120%+ NRR.
>
> 3. [System 3 — e.g., OKR framework]
>    Everyone was busy. Not everyone was aligned. Result: 85%+ objectives met for 5 consecutive cycles.
>
> The systems aren't the outcome. They're the enabler of the outcome.
>
> What's the first system you'd build at a new company?"

**Post Type 2: "Contrarian take" (Thought leadership)**
> "Most companies don't need a better strategy. They need better operating rhythms.
>
> I've seen 5-person companies with beautiful 5-year plans collecting dust. And 200-person companies that execute flawlessly on a napkin.
>
> The difference? Operating rhythm, not strategic sophistication.
>
> The companies that execute well do 3 things:
> • They have a clear weekly leadership meeting that drives decisions, not updates
> • They have quarterly planning that connects strategy to resourcing
> • They review metrics, not feelings
>
> Strategy is easy. Execution is a system.
>
> What's one operating rhythm that transformed your team?"

**Post Type 3: "Observation" (Industry insight)**
> "I've noticed something about companies crossing 50 people.
>
> The founders who were great at 10 people suddenly struggle at 50. They're still doing the same things that worked — and that's exactly the problem.
>
> The shift from 'founder does everything' to 'CEO leads through systems' is the hardest transition in a company's life.
>
> And it's the most important one.
>
> Because if you don't build the system, the system builds itself — and it's usually chaos."

**Post Type 4: "Advice" (Helpful)**
> "Getting your first COO or Chief of Staff? Here's what to look for:
>
> Don't look for someone who's done your exact job. Look for someone who's built systems from scratch.
>
> Because your playbook hasn't been written yet. And you need someone who writes playbooks — not someone who follows them.
>
> The best operators I know share 3 traits:
> • They see patterns where others see chaos
> • They build infrastructure that outlasts them
> • They make the CEO's job easier, not harder
>
> What's the #1 thing you'd look for?"
