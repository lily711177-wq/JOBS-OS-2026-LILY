# FIRST 90 DAYS ONBOARDING
## Because Getting the Job Is Step 1 — Keeping It Is Step 2

---

### WEEK 1: LISTEN & LEARN

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| 1 | Onboarding paperwork, IT setup, meet your team |
| 2-3 | 1:1s with every direct report (what's working, what's broken) |
| 4-5 | 1:1s with cross-functional peers (what do you need from me?) |

**Week 1 output:**
- [ ] Org chart memorized
- [ ] Key systems access (email, Slack, CRM, etc.)
- [ ] 15-minute 1:1 booked with each stakeholder
- [ ] List of current team priorities

---

### WEEK 2-4: DIAGNOSE

- Review current processes, metrics, and team structure
- Identify top 3 pain points (ask everyone: "What breaks most often?")
- Don't change anything yet. Just observe.
- Build relationships with your team members

**Week 4 output:**
- [ ] Current state assessment document
- [ ] Top 3 priorities identified
- [ ] Team strengths and gaps understood
- [ ] Relationship built with each direct report

---

### WEEK 5-8: FIRST MOVES

- Fix 1 obvious problem (quick win — builds credibility)
- Communicate your 90-day plan to your manager
- Start 1 strategic initiative (not 3 — focus)
- Set up regular 1:1 cadence with your team

**Week 8 output:**
- [ ] 1 quick win delivered
- [ ] 90-day plan shared with manager
- [ ] Regular team cadence established
- [ ] 1 strategic initiative scoped and started

---

### WEEK 9-12: BUILD MOMENTUM

- First strategic initiative showing results
- Team rhythm established (weekly, monthly, quarterly)
- Cross-functional relationships solid
- First pass at metrics/KPIs defined

**Week 12 output:**
- [ ] Strategic initiative on track for delivery
- [ ] Team operating rhythm running without you
- [ ] Manager review: "exceeds expectations"
- [ ] Next 90 days planned

---

### ONBOARDING MINDSET

1. **"Listen more than you speak"** — especially in the first month
2. **"Don't change what works"** — even if you'd do it differently
3. **"Quick wins, then deep work"** — build trust before transformation
4. **"Over-communicate early"** — set the tone for transparency
5. **"Find your allies"** — identify who wants you to succeed
