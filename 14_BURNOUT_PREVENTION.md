# BURNOUT PREVENTION & SUSTAINABILITY
## This Is a Marathon, Not a Sprint

---

### THE RISK
- 3.75 hours of high-cognitive-intensity work every morning
- 90%+ rejection rate on cold applications
- 7-day sprints with no break
- Financial pressure ($1200/month survival)
- PR timeline anxiety
- Emotional toll of constant rejection

**This is a recipe for burnout by week 3 without a sustainability system.**

---

### DAILY MINDSET RITUAL (5 Minutes Before Phase 1)

**Step 1: Breathe (1 min)**
4 seconds in → 4 seconds hold → 4 seconds out. Repeat 5x.

**Step 2: One sentence (1 min)**
Say out loud: *"I moved to Canada with nothing. I built a $17M company. Today I build the next chapter."*

**Step 3: One priority (1 min)**
What is the ONE application that matters most today? Not all 3. The one.

**Step 4: One release (1 min)**
What's one worry I'm carrying? Write it down. Close the notebook. It stays there.

**Step 5: Go (1 min)**
Start Phase 1.

### DAILY BOUNDARIES

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 6:00 AM - 9:45 AM | Job search (Phases 1-7) |
| 9:45 AM - 10:00 AM | Close loop. Save tracking. Close all tabs. |
| 10:00 AM onwards | Live your life. No job search. |
| After 8:00 PM | No job search. No LinkedIn. No email. |

**Hard rules:**
- No job search after 8 PM. Period.
- No checking email/LinkedIn before morning ritual.
- No applications on Sunday. NON-NEGOTIABLE. System enforced.
- One full day off per week.
- HARD RULE: If Sunday breaks, Monday's output drops 60%. Rest is a force multiplier.

---

### REJECTION RECOVERY (When It Hits Hard)

**Immediate:** 
- 5 minutes to feel it. Say "that one stung."
- Log it in tracking sheet (data point)
- One positive action (send one more app, or close the laptop and go outside)

**24-hour rule:**
- Don't make any decisions about your strategy when you're rejected
- Wait 24 hours before changing anything
- Most "I should lower my standards" thoughts disappear after 24 hours

**Weekly check-in (Friday 9:45 AM):**
- How many rejections this week?
- How many interviews?
- What did I learn?
- Am I still on track?
- What's one thing I'm grateful for?

### SOCIAL ACCOUNTABILITY

**Who holds you accountable?**
- One friend/family member who checks in weekly
- Share your weekly goal with them
- They ask: "How many applications this week? How many interviews?"
- They celebrate wins with you
- They remind you who you are after rejections

**If you don't have someone:**
- Write a weekly "progress report" anyway
- Read it to yourself out loud
- Track your own metrics

---

### WARNING SIGNS (Emergency Reset Needed)

| Sign | Action |
|------|--------|
| Skipping morning ritual 2+ days | Take a full day off. No job search. Reset. |
| Feeling dread before starting Phase 1 | Reduce daily target to 1 application. Momentum > volume. |
| Comparing yourself to others | Close LinkedIn. Go outside. Remember your journey is unique. |
| "I'll take anything" thoughts | Stop. Re-read 01_MASTER_CORPUS.md. Remember what you've built. |
| Physical symptoms (headaches, insomnia, fatigue) | Take 2 full days off. Health first. Jobs will be there. |

---

### THE LONG GAME

**Week 1-2:** Building momentum. Applications go out. Few responses.
**Week 3-4:** First interviews start trickling in.
**Week 5-6:** Interview cycles active. Some rejections, some advances.
**Week 7-8:** First offers or near-offers.
**Week 9-10:** Negotiation. Acceptance. Onboarding.

This timeline is realistic. Anything faster is a bonus. Anything slower is normal.

**You survived on $1200/month in Vancouver. You can survive a 10-week job search.**
