# NETWORKING STRATEGY
## Proactive — Not Reactive. Build Relationships Before You Need Them.

---

### THE 80/20 RULE

- 80% of executive roles are filled through networks, not cold applications
- 20% come from applying cold

Your current system is 100% cold applications. **Fix this now.**

---

### WEEKLY NETWORKING TARGETS

| Activity | Target | Time Investment |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| Coffee / video calls with connections | 3/week | 3 hours |
| LinkedIn posts (thought leadership) | 2/week | 1 hour |
| Industry events (in-person or virtual) | 1/week | 2 hours |
| New LinkedIn connections (quality) | 20/week | 30 min |
| Executive recruiter outreach | 2/week | 15 min |

---

### LINKEDIN CONNECTION STRATEGY

**Who to connect with:**
- Alumni from your schools (KPU, MBA program, BSc IT)
- People with "Recruiter" or "Talent" in title at target companies
- People with "Director of Operations" or similar title at target companies
- Vancouver-based COOs, VPs of Operations, Heads of Strategy
- Executive recruiters specializing in operations/strategy

**Connection request template:**
> "Hi [Name] — I'm an operations leader in Vancouver building my network. Would be great to connect."

**After they accept (T+2 days):**
> "Thanks for connecting. I'm exploring the [target industry] space — would you be open to a 15-minute chat about your experience?"

---

### EXECUTIVE RECRUITER OUTREACH

**Who:**
- Search for "Executive Recruiter" "Vancouver" on LinkedIn
- Look for agencies: Boyden, Caldwell Partners, Leaders International, LHH Knightsbridge, Odgers Berndtson
- In-house recruiters at target companies (Talent Acquisition, HR Business Partners)

**Email template:**
> **Subject:** Operations leader (former COO, scaled 3→120 → $17M exit)
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm an operations executive looking for my next role in Vancouver. I spent 8 years scaling a B2B SaaS from 3→120 people through a $17M exit — building every operational system from scratch.
>
> I'm targeting Director/VP-level roles in operations, strategy, or revenue operations at growth-stage companies.
>
> Do you work with any clients who need someone who's built the operational infrastructure for a scaling company? Happy to share my background in more detail.
>
> Best,
> Aryan

### INDUSTRY EVENTS

**Vancouver-based events to attend:**
- Vancouver Startup Week
- BC Tech Association events
- SaaS North (Vancouver)
- Vancouver Enterprise Forum
- Meetup groups: Operations, Strategy, SaaS
- YVR / Vancity / lululemon career events

**At each event:**
1. Have your 30-second pitch ready: "I help scaling companies build their operational infrastructure."
2. Collect 3-5 business cards / LinkedIn connections
3. Follow up within 24 hours: "Great meeting you at [event]. Would love to continue the conversation."

---

### 30-SECOND ELEVATOR PITCH

> "I help companies that are crossing from growth-stage to scale build their operational infrastructure. I spent 8 years doing exactly that — taking a company from 3 people to 120 through a $17M exit. Now I'm looking for the next company that needs someone to build their operating system."

**Why this works:**
- No industry mention (agnostic)
- Clear value proposition
- Specific, memorable metric
- Opens conversation for "What does that look like?"
