# AGNOSTIC FRAMING ENGINE
## How to Position for ANY Industry Without Industry Baggage

---

### THE PROBLEM
Aryan's entire career is in US healthcare B2B SaaS. When applying to:
- Consulting firms (EY, BDO) → "Why does this guy work in healthcare?"
- Retail (Aritzia) → "Does this guy understand retail?"
- Telco (Telus) → "Does this guy understand telecom?"
- Fintech (Procurify) → "Is this guy a finance person?"

### THE SOLUTION
Abstract every achievement to its **universal business function**. Industry becomes irrelevant. Capability becomes the story.

---

### FRAMING PRINCIPLES

| Principle | How |
|-----------|-----|
| **Lead with function, not context** | "Scaled operations 3→120" not "Scaled healthcare company" |
| **Use process language** | "Built compliance infrastructure" not "Built HIPAA compliance" |
| **Emphasize transferable metrics** | "$0→$4M revenue, $17M exit" works in any industry |
| **Mention industry only at the end** | "B2B SaaS, healthcare vertical" as a footnote |
| **Speak to THEIR pain** | Every company needs scaling, systems, revenue ops |

### INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC FRAMING ADJUSTMENTS

**For Consulting (EY, BDO, Deloitte):**
- Emphasize: "Built systems from scratch" = consulting skills
- Use: "client work", "stakeholder management", "deliverables"
- De-emphasize: single-company career

**For Retail (Aritzia):**
- Emphasize: "operational excellence", "process optimization", "scale"
- Use: "customer experience", "workplace strategy", "cross-functional"
- Add: "Passionate about [their industry]" in cover letter

**For Telco (Telus):**
- Emphasize: "complex operations", "regulatory compliance", "scale"
- Use: "network operations" (metaphorical), "customer-facing"
- De-emphasize: healthcare specifics

**For SaaS/Tech (Jobber, Boomi, Procurify):**
- Emphasize: Everything is directly transferable
- Use: "B2B SaaS" prominently
- They already speak the same language

**For Non-Profit/Government (VCH):**
- Emphasize: "compliance", "mission-driven", "stakeholder management"
- Use: "public service", "community impact"
- De-emphasize: "exit", "ARR", "revenue growth"

### PERCEPTION ENGINE — 3-MODE TONE & LANGUAGE GOVERNANCE

Every achievement can be framed three ways. Choose the mode based on your target sector, not your personal style. The facts stay the same — the language changes.

| Mode | Target Sectors | Core Tone | Power Words | Avoid Words |
|------|---------------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Authentic** | Public sector, healthcare providers, government, compliance-heavy orgs | Professional, process-oriented, stewardship | Governance, Compliance, SOP, Stakeholder Management, Process Improvement, Accountability, Patient Safety | Disruption, Alpha, Aggressive, Hustle |
| **Hybrid** | Big 4, MBB, Corporate Strategy, FAANG, Telco (Telus) | Strategic, consultative, transformational | Framework, Scalability, P&L, Go-to-Market, Change Management, Cross-Functional Leadership, Strategic Alignment | Grinding, Hustling, Cutting Corners |
| **Machiavellian** | Startups, boutique firms, BD-heavy roles, high-growth tech | Bold, results-driven, velocity-focused | Alpha, Revenue Growth, Market Capture, Execution Velocity, Competitive Edge, Deal Flow, Growth Hacking | Bureaucracy, Slow, Process-Heavy |

### CAPABILITIES MATRIX (Titles Are a Charade)

Forget the role title. Extract the company's NEEDS from the JD, then map your capabilities to those needs. If 3+ needs match, you're in — regardless of what they call the role.

#### Your Capability Buckets

| Capability | What It Maps To | Evidence |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **Org Scaling** | Building teams, hiring infrastructure, onboarding at speed | 3→120 employees, sub-30-day ramp, 95% retention |
| **Revenue Ops** | CRM architecture, pipeline forecasting, NRR improvement | $0→$4M ARR, 40% sales cycle reduction, 120%+ NRR |
| **Strategic Planning** | OKRs, board reporting, financial modeling, 3-year planning | 5 OKR cycles, 85% attainment, $200K savings |
| **M&A / Exit** | Due diligence, integration, talent retention | $17M exit, 8 workstreams, 100% talent retention |
| **Compliance Systems** | Regulatory frameworks, audit readiness, SOP design | 100% audit pass rate, enterprise-grade compliance |
| **Cross-Functional Leadership** | Aligning engineering, sales, customer success, finance | 12 departments, remote-first operating model |
| **Process Design** | Workflow optimization, system automation, efficiency gains | 40% onboarding reduction, 25% billing error reduction |
| **Advisory/Consulting** | Diagnosing problems, building solutions, stepping back | Post-acquisition advisory engagements |

#### How to Use

1. **Read the JD. Ignore the title.**
2. **Extract 5-7 needs** the company has (e.g., "needs to standardize operations across teams", "needs revenue forecasting")
3. **Map each need to your capability buckets** above
4. **If 3+ map cleanly** → APPLY. Title irrelevant.
5. **If 1-2 map** → strategic positioning needed. Skip if none.
6. **Your resume/cover letter leads with the NEEDS you solve**, not the title they're hiring for.

#### Example

| JD Says | Company's Real Need | Your Capability | Match |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------|-------|
| "Director of something" | Needs someone to build cross-functional processes | Cross-Functional Leadership | ✅ |
| "5+ years in industry" | Needs someone who has scaled before | Org Scaling | ✅ |
| "Stakeholder management" | Needs someone who can align exec team | Strategic Planning | ✅ |

The title is "Director [Whatever]." The match is three capabilities. You apply.
