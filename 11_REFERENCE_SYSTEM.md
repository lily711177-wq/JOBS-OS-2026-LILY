# REFERENCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
## Every Reference Must Tell the Same Story You Tell

---

### THE PROBLEM
Your resume says: "Operations executive who scaled B2B SaaS 3→120, built every system from scratch."

Your references (CEO, colleagues) knew you in a healthcare SaaS context. If they say: "Aryan ran our healthcare software operations" — the agnostic framing collapses.

**Solution:** Brief every reference before they're called.

---

### REFERENCE BRIEFING DOCUMENT (Send to Each Reference)

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm interviewing for [Company] and they'll likely reach out for a reference. Here's how I'm positioning myself so there's no confusion:
>
> **My positioning:** "Operations executive who scaled a B2B SaaS company from 3→120 people through a $17M exit — building every operational system from scratch (hiring, revenue ops, compliance, strategy)."
>
> **What I'd appreciate you highlighting:**
> - The scale journey: 3 people to 120 in 24 months
> - The systems I built: HR infrastructure, OKRs, revenue operations, financial models
> - The exit: $17M acquisition, I led due diligence + integration
>
> **Healthcare context:** Only mention it if asked directly. If they ask what industry, say "B2B SaaS, healthcare vertical" — keeping "SaaS" as the primary frame.
>
> **One specific story that would help:** [Customize per reference — e.g., "the time I built the hiring pipeline from scratch" or "how I managed the due diligence process"]
>
> Thanks for your support. Let me know if you have questions.

### REFERENCE TYPES TO PREPARE

**Type 1: Direct Manager (CEO)**
Best for: Leadership, strategic thinking, overall performance
Story to tell: 3→120 scaling, how you partnered with CEO

**Type 2: Peer/Department Head**
Best for: Cross-functional collaboration, team building
Story to tell: Specific initiative you led together, how you handle conflict

**Type 3: Direct Report (Optional)**
Best for: Management style, mentoring, culture building
Story to tell: How you developed them, your leadership approach

---

### RED FLAGS TO ADDRESS WITH REFERENCES

| Potential Issue | How to Preview with Reference |
|----------------|------------------------------|
| "Aryan worked in healthcare" | "If they ask about industry, say 'B2B SaaS, healthcare vertical' — keep the emphasis on SaaS" |
| "The company was small" | "Frame it as 'high-growth scale-up' not 'small company'" |
| "The exit was an acquisition" | "Frame it as 'successful exit' and 'strategic integration'" |
| "He was there 8 years" | "Frame as 'deep ownership and commitment' not 'stayed too long'" |

### POST-OFFER REFERENCE FOLLOW-UP

After you accept:
> "Thank you so much for your reference. I accepted the offer at [Company]. Couldn't have done it without your support. Let me know how I can return the favor."

This maintains the relationship for your next move.
