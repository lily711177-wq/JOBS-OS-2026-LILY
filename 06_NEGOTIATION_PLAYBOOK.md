# MACHIAVELLIAN OFFER NEGOTIATION PLAYBOOK
## No Mercy. No Remorse. Pure Precision.

---

### CORE PRINCIPLES

1. **Never accept the first offer.** Ever. It's their floor, not their ceiling.
2. **Anchor high.** 110% of posted max. If range is $140-180K, ask for $198K.
3. **Create leverage.** "I have other interviews at similar stages" (always true).
4. **Silence is a weapon.** After stating your number, shut up. First person to speak loses.
5. **Be willing to walk.** Desperation smells. You survived on $1200/mo — you can survive longer.

---

### THE 5-STAGE NEGOTIATION

#### Stage 1: The First Call (HR Screen)

**Their move:** "What's your salary expectation?"
**Your move:** Deflect.
> "I'd like to understand the role deeper before discussing numbers. What's the budgeted range for this position?"

**If they insist:**
> "Based on my experience scaling a company 3→120 through a $17M exit, I'd expect a total package in the $180-200K range. But I'm flexible for the right opportunity."

#### Stage 2: The Offer Call

**Their move:** "We'd like to offer you $X."
**Your move:** Silence (3-5 seconds). Then:
> "I appreciate the offer. Based on my conversations with the team and the scope of this role, I was expecting something in the range of [110% of posted max]. Is there flexibility on compensation?"

**Then: SHUT UP. Count to 10 in your head. Let them fill the silence.**

#### Stage 3: The Pushback

**Their move:** "That's at the top of our range / we need to check / that's not possible."
**Your move:** Create leverage + value reminder:
> "I understand. Let me share why I'm worth that investment. [Recite 1 key achievement from this company's perspective — e.g., "I reduced sales cycle by 40% — even a 10% improvement for your team would justify this difference."] Also, I'm currently in conversations with [name-drop similar-tier company if possible]. Can we get closer to my range?"

#### Stage 4: The Compromise

**Their move:** "We can do $X + bonus/equity/perks."
**Your move:** Assess if it's enough. If yes:
> "I can make that work if we add [one specific thing — signing bonus, extra week vacation, remote flexibility, professional development budget, guaranteed first review in 6 months]."

**Rule:** Always get ONE extra thing. It establishes that you negotiate.

#### Stage 5: The Acceptance

> "I appreciate your partnership on this. I'm excited to join [Company] and start delivering. You can expect the official acceptance signed by [end of day tomorrow]."

### SPECIFIC SCRIPTS

#### When they say "What's your current salary?"
> "My previous role was equity-heavy as a COO at a growth-stage company. My base was $[X], but my total compensation including bonus and exit was significantly higher. I'm focused on finding the right role now — can you share your range?"

#### When they say "We have other candidates at lower cost"
> "I understand budget constraints. I'd just ask you to consider the ROI I bring. If I reduce your sales cycle by even 20%, that alone covers my compensation multiple times over. Let's find a number that works for both of us."

#### When they say "This is our final offer"
> "I respect that. Let me take 24 hours to review the full package and come back to you tomorrow."

**Then wait 24 hours. Accept or counter once more.**

### COMPETING OFFER STRATEGY

**How to create and leverage competing offers:**

**Setup (Before you have any offer):**
- Stagger applications so top-3 companies' interview timelines overlap
- Target: Company A (most desired), Company B (good backup), Company C (decent)
- Delay Company A's final round until Company B and C are at offer stage

**When you have 1 offer:**
> "I'm really excited about [Company A]. I want to be transparent — I have another offer that I'm evaluating with a deadline of [date]. Is it possible to get your decision by then?"

**When you have 2 offers:**
> "I'm fortunate to have two strong options. [Company B] has offered [number]. I prefer [Company A] for [reason], but the gap is significant. Can you work on narrowing it?"

**When stalling:**
1. "I need to review the full offer package — can I get back to you in 48 hours?"
2. "I have a final-round interview scheduled next week that I want to complete before deciding."
3. "My [immigration lawyer / accountant] needs to review the offer."

**Never lie about having another offer. But you can:**
- Accelerate Company B's timeline (ask them to expedite)
- Delay Company A's response (ask for more time to "review")
- Create genuine competitive tension without fabrication

#### Offer Acceptance & Rejection Letters

**Acceptance:**
> Dear [Name],
>
> Thank you for the offer to join [Company] as [Role]. After careful consideration, I'm delighted to accept.
>
> I confirm the terms discussed:
> - Base salary: $[amount]
> - Annual bonus: [%]
> - Start date: [date]
>
> I'm excited to get started and contribute to [Company]'s next chapter. Please let me know the next steps for onboarding documentation.
>
> Best,
> Aryan

**Rejection (keep the door open):**
> Dear [Name],
>
> Thank you so much for the offer and your time throughout this process. After careful consideration, I've decided to accept another opportunity that aligns more closely with my current priorities.
>
> I have tremendous respect for [Company] and the team. If there's a way to stay in touch for future opportunities, I'd appreciate that.
>
> Thank you again for your consideration.
>
> Best,
> Aryan

#### Red Flags That Mean Walk Away
1. They refuse to put offer in writing
2. They pressure you to decide same day
3. They lowball by more than 30% from your ask
4. The role description changed during interviews
5. Multiple people said "we're like a family"
